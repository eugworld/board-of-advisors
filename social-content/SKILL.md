# Social Media Content Creation Skill

## Setup

Before generating any content, reference:
- **persona.md** — Your brand voice, target audience, professional background, and content themes
- **CHALLENGE_PROTOCOL.md** — Your framework for validation and iteration

This skill generates production-ready content immediately. When you say "write me a LinkedIn post about X," you'll get 2-3 versions with different hooks, ready to post.

## 1. Before Creating Content (Context Gathering)

Quick checklist before generating. Keep it lightweight—ask only what's needed.

**What I'll confirm:**
- **Goal**: Awareness? Engagement? Conversion? Community building?
- **Audience**: Who's this for? (target persona, seniority level, industry)
- **Angle**: What's the unique take? (personal story, contrarian, data-driven, how-to, mistake)
- **Resources**: Any assets? (images, videos, links, stats, case studies)
- **Brand voice**: Reference persona.md for tone consistency

**Example confirmation:**
"I want to write a LinkedIn post about productivity systems. Audience: early-stage founders. Angle: the system I'm actually using (not the one I tell people about). Goal: engagement and DMs from founders wanting to discuss systems. Reference my productivity tool experience."

## 2. Platform Strategy Guide

### LinkedIn
**Why it works**: Professional network where transparency and personal narrative outperform corporate messaging.

**Algorithm tips:**
- Posts with 2-4 line breaks and emoji outlier detection. Capitalize on curiosity and specificity.
- Dwell time matters—hook them in the first line, make them want to click "see more"
- Shares > likes in terms of reach. Write invitations for discussion.
- Native content outperforms links in the body (link in comments instead)
- Video gets 3x engagement. Carousel posts (image series) get 2-3x engagement

**Frequency**: 3-5x per week (or 2-3x if quality-focused)

**What works:**
- Personal stories (transformative moments, failures, realizations)
- Contrarian takes (challenge conventional wisdom respectfully)
- How-I-did-it posts (specific processes, systems, frameworks)
- Data-driven insights (share numbers, patterns, trends from your work)
- Lessons learned (mistakes, pivots, course corrections)
- Behind-the-scenes (building in public, transparency)

**What doesn't work:**
- Promotional posts ("Check out my product!")
- Corporate speak ("Excited to announce...")
- Vague wisdom ("Success takes hard work")
- Link drops without context
- Engagement bait ("Like this if you agree")

**First line is everything.** That's what shows on the feed. Make them click "see more."

### Twitter/X
**Why it works**: Real-time, fast-moving, rewards hot takes, commentary, and strong opinions.

**Algorithm tips:**
- Tweets under 100 characters get 40% more engagement
- Threading is powerful—each tweet should stand alone AND add to the narrative
- Quote tweets with original insight beat plain retweets 10:1
- Emoji use increases engagement by 25%, but use sparingly (1-2 max per tweet)
- Reply immediately after posting to amplify (algorithm boost)

**Frequency**: 1-3 times daily (quality over volume)

**What works:**
- Hot takes with reasoning (not just opinions)
- Real-time commentary on industry news
- Threads that teach (step-by-step, frameworks, analysis)
- Questions that invite discussion
- Specific wins/metrics ("2,000 new subscribers this month")
- Self-deprecating humor (human, relatable)

**What doesn't work:**
- Vague positivity
- Over-promotional
- Threads that are too long (thread threshold: 8-12 tweets max)

**Format winning pattern:** Hook (grab attention) → Value/story (deliver) → CTA (invite response)

---

### Instagram
**Why it works**: Visual-first, community-driven, rewards consistency and storytelling.

**Algorithm tips:**
- Reels get 2x reach compared to photos
- Carousel posts (2-10 image series) with educational content perform exceptionally
- Saves indicate content quality—optimize for saving (actionable, valuable, beautiful)
- Comments matter more than likes
- Captions with line breaks get better dwell time
- Use 3-5 hashtags (research ones with 100K-500K followers, not 1M+)

**Frequency**: 2-4x per week (posts + reels + stories)

**What works:**
- Reels: educational, entertaining, or behind-the-scenes (under 60 seconds, native audio/trending)
- Carousels: before/after, step-by-step guides, lists (5-10 slides ideal)
- Stories: daily, authentic, unpolished (polls, questions, announcements)
- Captions: storytelling, vulnerability, specific examples
- Call-outs to other creators (builds community)

**Caption formula:** Hook → Story/value → CTA (save, follow, DM, comment)

---

### TikTok
**Why it works**: Algorithm rewards native, unpolished content; native audio and trends are essential.

**Algorithm tips:**
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds or you lose them
- Native TikTok audio dramatically outperforms imported audio
- Video length: 15-34 seconds ideal (under 60s for new accounts)
- Vertical only—no horizontal
- Captions (burnt-in text) boost engagement
- Watch time and completion rate are THE metrics

**Frequency**: 3-5x per week minimum (algorithm favors consistent creators)

**What works:**
- Educational (quick tips, tricks, how-to)
- Day-in-the-life (behind the scenes, routine)
- Tool/resource reviews ("This app changed how I...")
- Trend-jacking (your spin on trending sound/format)
- Storytelling (personal narrative, short format)
- Reaction videos (with context, not just reacting)

**What doesn't work:**
- Over-produced (looks inauthentic)
- Horizontal videos
- No trend adoption (you're invisible without native audio)
- No captions
- Poor lighting

**Format winning pattern:** Hook 0-2s → Setup 2-5s → Value/story 5-25s → CTA 25-30s

---

### Facebook
**Why it works**: Reach older demographics; communities and groups are strong; algorithm favors engagement and shares.

**Algorithm tips:**
- Native video outperforms links
- Groups > Pages for engagement
- Live video gets 10x engagement
- Longer-form posts (250+ words) get more dwell time
- Local content performs exceptionally

**Frequency**: 1-2x per day in communities; 3-4x per week on page

**What works:**
- Community building (groups for your niche)
- Live streams (Q&A, tutorials, announcements)
- Native video (uploads beat YouTube links)
- Longer narratives with emotional hooks
- Local and relevant content
- Asking questions and responding to every comment

## 3. Content Pillars Framework

Content pillars = recurring themes that define your expertise and voice.

**Why pillars matter:**
- Consistency in audience expectations
- Easier batching and planning
- Clearer personal brand
- Better algorithm performance (signals expertise)

### Building Your Pillars (3-5 total)

Each pillar should be:
- A distinct category you can speak to credibly
- Aligned with your expertise and audience needs
- Sustainable for 3+ months without burning out
- Differentiated (not all overlapping)

### Example Breakdown (For a Solopreneur):

**Pillar 1: Systems & Productivity (30%)**
- How I organize my work
- Tools I use and why
- Mistakes I've made with systems
- Templates and frameworks

**Pillar 2: Building in Public (25%)**
- Business metrics and numbers
- Behind-the-scenes stories
- Lessons from mistakes
- Growth insights

**Pillar 3: Contrarian Thoughts (25%)**
- Challenges to conventional wisdom
- Unpopular opinions backed by data
- What everyone gets wrong about X
- Industry hot takes

**Pillar 4: Audience Help (20%)**
- Direct advice and how-to
- Q&A responses
- Problem-solving content
- Actionable frameworks

### Planning Content Mix

**Weekly allocation** (across all platforms):
- 30% Pillar 1
- 25% Pillar 2
- 25% Pillar 3
- 20% Pillar 4

**Monthly check:** Are you hitting your pillar percentages? Adjust if one pillar feels stale.

## 4. Post Templates & Formats (THE CORE)

### LinkedIn Post Templates

#### Template 1: The Story Post
*Use when: You have a personal transformation, lesson, or moment of realization.*

```
[HOOK - curiosity or specific moment]
I was [situation] when [event].

[SCENE - make them see/feel it]
I remember [specific detail]. [Another detail].

[CHALLENGE - the conflict]
Then I realized [problem]. [consequence if unchanged].

[TURNING POINT - the shift]
That's when I [action/realization].

[RESULT - tangible outcome]
[Timeframe later], [specific result]. [number/metric if possible].

[LESSON - the takeaway]
The lesson: [principle]. This changed how I [think/do] X.

[QUESTION - invite engagement]
Have you experienced [related situation]? What did you learn?
```

**Example:**
```
I was burned out. Completely.

I remember staring at my calendar at 10 PM, realizing I'd taken 47 calls that week. Each one felt important. Each one drained me.

The problem: I'd said yes to everyone. Meetings felt like a status symbol—the busier you were, the more important you seemed.

That's when I made the shift. I decided to say no to 80% of new meetings. Built a 10-minute "office hours" window instead.

Three months later: same revenue, 25 fewer meetings, and I'd actually shipped 3 new features instead of talking about them.

The lesson: Busy doesn't mean important. Calendar control is life control.

How many meetings are on YOUR calendar that don't deserve to be there?
```

Instead: Find an intersection of (something you're decent at) × (something people will pay for) × (something that energizes you, at least 40%). Then commit for 3-5 years.

**Example:**
```

2. Short sentences. Land. One idea. Per line. It increases dwell time because your brain processes faster.

3. Vulnerability beats perfection. Posts about my failures (I've lost $47K) get 3x engagement vs. wins.

4. Questions at the end matter. Comments signal value to the algorithm. A post with 20 comments and 100 likes will reach 10x more people.

5. Consistency beats virality. One viral post teaches you nothing. 50 consistent posts build a reputation and audience.

The mistake: Trying to make every post viral. That's not how this works. Show up 50 times, and you'll eventually hit viral posts naturally.

Which of these will you test first?
```

**Example:**
```
How I used to run client calls vs. how I run them now. The difference is striking.

- 1-hour calls with no agenda
- "Let's just see what comes up"
- Multiple meetings per week (Tuesday, Thursday, Friday)
- Client didn't send prep notes
- I answered everything in real-time

NEW WAY:

- Client sends two specific questions 24 hours before
- I answer prepared; we focus on decisions
- Result: Clear action items, 3x faster project completion

Why the new way works:
1. Constraints force clarity. A 30-min call with an agenda is 3x more focused than a rambling 1-hour call.
2. Less friction. One meeting every 2 weeks beats 3 scattered meetings. I'm in flow longer; client has continuity.

When I switched: Same client revenue. Projects shipped 3 weeks faster on average.

**Example:**
```

Why I made it: I thought asking was pushy. Good work speaks for itself. I confused confidence with silence.

The root cause: Scarcity mindset. If I ask, they might say no. Better to not ask. (Terrible logic.)

Now I ask for everything. "Here's the work. Here's the next step I recommend." "I'm raising my rate to $X because of these value additions." "I'd love a referral—here's what kind of client is ideal."

Applied this 47 times this year. Result: 3 referrals, 7 rate increases, 12 follow-on projects. Probably an extra $80K in revenue I never would have gotten.

```

**Example:**
```

Tweet 4: That middle tier feels reasonable by comparison. It's not actually cheaper—it FEELS cheaper relative to the anchor price.

Tweet 5: They're also signaling: this product is serious. It costs money. You're not the customer—you're a partner paying for utility.

Tweet 6: Free users become paid users faster when there's a premium tier they're "upgrading past." Psychology over economics.

Tweet 7: They also build in upgrade momentum. Month 1: free. Month 6: paid tier. Year 2: premium tier. The ladder is built in.

Tweet 8: What you can steal: Build 3 pricing tiers. Make the middle tier the obvious choice. Use the top tier as an anchor. Watch conversion rates jump.

## 5. Hook Formulas (Critical Section)

The hook is your only real estate on the feed. Master these patterns and you'll 10x your reach.

### Curiosity Hooks

```
"I was wrong about..."
"The real reason [belief everyone has] is false"
"Nobody talks about..."
"I've been [practicing X] wrong for [timeframe]"
"The mistake that cost me [amount/consequence]"
"Here's what everyone gets wrong about [topic]"
"I thought [belief]. Then [event]. Everything changed."
```

**Examples:**
- "I was wrong about productivity. Here's what actually works."
- "The real reason most side hustles fail (and it's not what you think)."
- "Nobody talks about the worst part of going freelance."
- "I've been writing LinkedIn posts wrong for two years."
- "The mistake that cost me $50K in lost revenue."
- "Here's what everyone gets wrong about personal branding."
- "I thought remote work was the dream. Then I tried it."

---

### Story Hooks

```
"Last week, I [specific action/event]."
"I almost [major consequence]. Here's what I learned."
"3 years ago, I was [situation]. Now I'm [different situation]. Here's what changed."
"I remember the exact moment when [turning point]."
"There was a time when [past struggle]."
```

**Examples:**
- "Last week, a client fired me. Best thing that happened."
- "I almost quit my business. But then I realized something."
- "3 years ago, I was broke with a failing side project. Now it's $100K ARR. Here's the 5 pivots that saved it."
- "I remember the exact moment I decided to quit my job."
- "There was a time when I refreshed my Twitter waiting for engagement. Sounds pathetic now."

---

### Value Hooks

```
"Here's how to [outcome] in [timeframe]"
"[Number] things that [benefit]"
"Stop doing [common mistake]. Do this instead."
"[Number] little-known ways to [benefit]"
"The fastest way to [outcome]"
"If you want to [goal], start by [action]"
```

**Examples:**
- "Here's how to write a LinkedIn post that gets 100+ comments."
- "5 systems that save me 10 hours/week."
- "Stop networking randomly. Do this instead."
- "3 little-known ways to get referrals."
- "The fastest way to go from 0 to 1,000 Twitter followers."
- "If you want to build an audience, start by writing daily."

---

### Contrarian Hooks

```
"Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]."
"[Everyone believes X]. The opposite is true."
"This is going to sound crazy, but [controversial take]."
"I'm going to say something controversial: [statement]."
"Don't [common advice]. Here's why."
```

**Examples:**
- "Unpopular opinion: You don't need a business degree."
- "Everyone believes hard work equals success. The opposite is true."
- "This is going to sound crazy, but you should charge more."
- "I'm going to say something controversial: Motivation is overrated."
- "Don't follow your passion. Find your intersection instead."

---

### Social Proof Hooks

```
"We achieved [outcome] in [timeframe]. Here's how."
"[Number] people asked me [question]. So I created [solution]."
"My team just [achievement]. Here's what we did."
"[Specific metric] increase in [timeframe]. Here's the system."
```

**Examples:**
- "We hit $1M ARR in 18 months. Here's our playbook."
- "50 people asked me how to grow on LinkedIn. So I built a framework."
- "My team closed 3 clients in one week using this one change."
- "300% revenue increase in 6 months. Here's the system."

---

### Question Hooks (Lower engagement, but can work)

```
"What if [thing everyone believes] was actually [opposite]?"
"Why do [problem] happen?"
"How many of you [relatable situation]?"
```

**Examples:**
- "What if the key to productivity wasn't working harder?"
- "Why do most creators give up in month 2?"
- "How many of you are working 60-hour weeks and still broke?"

*Note: Question hooks underperform statement hooks. Use sparingly.*

## 6. Content Repurposing System

One piece of content should fuel 5-7 pieces across platforms. This multiplies your effort.

### The Repurposing Framework

**Start with: 1 core idea** (blog post, video, insight, case study)

**Transform into:**
- 1 LinkedIn post (long-form)
- 1 Twitter thread (7-10 tweets)
- 3-5 Instagram posts (carousel + Reel + 1-2 Reels)
- 2-3 TikToks
- 1 email or newsletter excerpt
- Optional: Podcast episode, YouTube short

### Transformation Table: Blog Post → Multiple Platforms

**Original:** Blog post titled "How I Went From $0 to $100K ARR in 18 Months"

| Platform | Format | Transformation |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| LinkedIn | Single post | Extract biggest lesson. Add hook. Ask question. |
| Twitter | Thread (8 tweets) | Break each section into a tweet. Hook + 6 insights + CTA |
| Instagram Carousel | 8-slide visual | 1 hook slide + 6 lesson slides + 1 CTA slide |
| Instagram Reel | 30-second video | Show the before/after metric. Use trending audio. |
| TikTok #1 | 20-second video | The #1 thing that mattered. Hook first. |
| TikTok #2 | 25-second video | Common mistake breakdown. |
| TikTok #3 | 30-second video | Day-in-the-life during the journey. |

---

### Repurposing Templates

**From Blog Post to LinkedIn:**
- Extract the most surprising insight
- Add a personal hook ("I learned this the hard way")
- Rewrite for short paragraphs, line breaks
- Ask a question at the end
- Link to blog post in comments (not body)

**From Blog Post to Twitter Thread:**
- Intro tweet: State the outcome + promise
- Tweets 2-7: One insight per tweet, specific and actionable
- Closing tweet: Summary + CTA (link in reply, not thread)

**From Blog Post to Instagram Carousel:**
- Slide 1: Bold hook ("5 Things That 10x'd My Revenue")
- Slides 2-6: One insight per slide with visual (graphic, photo, or text overlay)
- Slide 7: Call-to-action (save, comment, DM)

**From Blog Post to TikTok:**
- Hook first 2 seconds
- Pick the most surprising insight
- Show, don't tell (screen recording, visual proof)
- Metric/proof in final seconds
- Invite comment

## 8. Engagement Strategy

Posting is 20% of the battle. Engagement is 80%.

### Daily Engagement Routine (30 minutes)

**Goal: Build relationships with 20-50 key creators in your space.**

**Workflow:**

1. **First 10 minutes**: Check replies/comments on your posts from the past 48 hours. Reply to every single one (even if it's just a thumb's up emoji or "Thank you!").

2. **Next 15 minutes**: Go to 10-15 of your target creators' recent posts. Read them. Leave a thoughtful comment (not "Great post!").
   - Find their top 5 posts from the past week
   - Click one that resonates
   - Write a comment that adds insight or asks a follow-up question
   - Example: "This approach reminds me of [related idea]. Have you tested [follow-up question]?"

3. **Final 5 minutes**: Retweet/reshare 2-3 posts with original commentary (add value, not just a reshare).

**Keys:**
- Quality comments > quantity of likes
- Comment early (first 2 hours) to get more visibility
- Tag the creator if you're asking a question
- Reply to comments you receive (conversation matters)

---

### Quality Comment Framework

Bad: "Great post!"
Good: "This reminds me of [related idea]. I'd add that [your insight] because [reason]. Have you tested [follow-up question]?"

**Structure:**
1. Relate to their idea (show you read it)
2. Add your own insight (provide value to their audience)
3. Ask a follow-up (continues conversation)

---

### Building Relationships

**Target 20-50 creators in your niche.**

- Follow them
- Engage with 2-3 posts per week from each
- Reply when they comment on yours
- DM thoughtfully (not "hey" or "let's collab")
- Mention them in content when relevant
- Celebrate their wins publicly

After 2-3 months of this: genuine relationship. They become audience, collaborators, referral sources.

---

### Handling Negative Comments

**Strategy:**

1. **Take a breath.** Don't reply immediately.

2. **Assess:** Is this feedback or trolling?
   - Feedback: Has a point (even if critical)
   - Trolling: Just wants to upset you

3. **Respond to feedback:** "You make a fair point. I hadn't considered [their point]. Thanks for the perspective."

4. **Ignore trolling:** Delete or mute. Don't feed it.

5. **Never:** Get defensive, sarcastic, or mean. Your audience is watching.

**Example:**
- Comment: "This advice is bad and you're overhyped."
- Your response: "I appreciate you pushing back. This approach works for my situation, but it's definitely not one-size-fits-all. What's worked better for you?"

This shows confidence and maturity. Observers respect it.

## 9. Reverse Engineering Viral Content (Masterclass Section)

"Going viral" is a byproduct of consistent value, not a strategy. But patterns exist.

### The 6-Step Reverse Engineering Framework

#### Step 1: NICHE ID
Identify 10-20 top creators in your niche who consistently get high engagement.

**Action:**
- Search your niche on LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, TikTok
- Find accounts with 10K-1M followers (sweet spot for engagement data)
- Ideal: 20-40% engagement rate (more realistic than mega-accounts)
- Document their names, handle, platform

**Example:** For "content creation" niche: @thealexhormozi, @neilpatel, @garyvee, @corymwatkins, etc.

---

#### Step 2: SCRAPE
Analyze 50+ posts from each creator. Look for patterns.

**Tools:** Screenshot, spreadsheet, or manual note-taking. Look at:
- Hook type (story, curiosity, value, contrarian, social proof)
- Format (carousel, thread, single post, video)
- Post length
- Call-to-action
- Engagement numbers
- Comments sentiment

**What you're hunting:** Which posts get the most engagement? What do the top 10% have in common?

---

#### Step 3: ANALYZE
Extract patterns from the top 10% of content.

**Questions to answer:**
- What hook types dominate the top posts?
- How long are the posts?
- What formats (thread vs. single post)?
- What's the tone? (Vulnerable? Bold? Data-driven?)
- How specific are they? (Names, numbers, dates vs. vague?)
- Where's the call-to-action?
- How many line breaks?

**Document:** Create a one-page summary of "what works" in your niche.

---

#### Step 4: PLAYBOOK
Codify the patterns into templates and rules.

**Example output:**

"In the productivity niche:
- Top posts use story hooks (60%)
- Average length: 150-250 words
- Always include 1-2 specific numbers ($47K, 3 months)
- Contrarian takes get 2x comments vs. tips
- CTA is always a question, never a link
- First line appears before 'see more' (LinkedIn) — must be specific, not vague
- Emojis: 1-3 used strategically, not scattered"

---

#### Step 5: LAYER VOICE
Apply direct response principles. Make it sound like YOU, not a template.

**Principles:**
- Write like a smart friend who figured something out
- Specific > vague ("I made $47,329" beats "I made good money")
- Short sentences. Land. Create. Breathe.
- Write from emotion first, then logic
- Show vulnerability (the struggling part, not just the win)

**Example:**
Instead of: "Through systematic productivity optimization, I achieved enhanced output."
Write: "I was drowning in tasks. Then I cut my meetings in half. Suddenly, I shipped things."

---

#### Step 6: CONVERT
Turn attention into action (soft or direct conversions).

**Soft conversions** (build audience first):
- "Reply with [something]"
- "Comment which one you'll try"
- "Save this for later"
- "Tag someone who needs this"

**Direct conversions** (after audience exists):
- "Link in bio"
- "DM me for [offer]"
- "Check out [product]"

*Timing:* Use soft conversions for months 1-6 (build trust). Then layer in direct conversions.

## 10. Voice Principles

Your voice is your competitive advantage. It's what makes people say, "This sounds like [you]."

### The "Smart Friend" Tone

Imagine you're texting advice to a friend who just asked for help. That's your voice.

**Characteristics:**
- You figured something out (not lecturing from on high)
- You're excited to share it (conversational, not academic)
- You acknowledge you might be wrong (humble, not dogmatic)
- You use specific examples (not abstract principles)
- You write like you talk (contractions, short sentences, line breaks)

---

### Key Principles

**1. Specific > Vague**

Vague: "I made good money"
Specific: "I made $47,329 in my first year. Broke $100K in year 3."

Vague: "Productivity tools changed my life"
Specific: "Switched from Asana to Notion. Cut my planning time from 4 hours to 1 hour per week."

Specific numbers, dates, names, and examples are 10x more believable and memorable.

---

**2. Short. Breathe. Land.**

Write one idea per sentence. Use line breaks like punctuation.

Bad:
"I realized through extensive research and personal experimentation that the most effective approach to building an audience on social media is to maintain consistent posting frequency, engage authentically with your target demographic, and focus on delivering genuine value rather than pursuing vanity metrics."

Good:
"Here's what actually works:
Post consistently.
Engage authentically.
Deliver value.
Ignore vanity metrics."

Line breaks force white space. Your reader's brain processes faster. Dwell time increases.

---

**3. Write from Emotion**

Don't start with logic. Start with the feeling.

Bad: "Networking is important because it creates professional opportunities."
Good: "I was terrified to reach out. What if they said no? But I did anyway. Three of those conversations turned into projects."

Emotion sticks. Logic fades. Lead with the emotional truth.

---

**4. Humble + Bold**

Be specific and confident about what you know. Be humble about what you don't.

"I've tested this 50 times. Consistent result: X. I might be wrong—these are my observations, not laws. Have you seen something different?"

Confidence + humility = trustworthy.

## 11. Analytics & Optimization

Track what matters. Ignore vanity metrics.

### Metrics That Matter

**Awareness (top of funnel):**
- Impressions (how many people saw it)
- Reach (unique people)
- Follower growth

**Engagement (middle of funnel):**
- Comments (highest quality signal)
- Shares (retweets, reposts)
- Click-through rate (if you're driving traffic)

**Conversion (bottom of funnel):**
- DMs (interest signal)
- Link clicks
- Sales (if applicable)

### Weekly Tracking Checklist

Every Sunday, spend 15 minutes reviewing:

**Questions:**
- What was my top performing post? Why? (hook, format, topic)
- What underperformed? Why?
- Did I post on schedule?
- Did I engage 30 min daily?
- What's my follower growth? (target: 2-5% weekly)
- Which pillar had the best engagement?

**Action:** If a format/topic crushes it, do more of it next week.

---

### Optimization Actions

**For low engagement:**
- Revisit hooks (are they specific enough?)
- Check posting time (posting when audience is offline?)
- Audit CTAs (are you inviting response?)
- Evaluate format (maybe video instead of carousel)

**For declining reach:**
- Are you posting consistently? (Algorithm punishes sporadic posting)
- Has your topic drifted from your pillar?
- Is your engagement routine slipping? (Post-to-engagement ratio matters)
- Test new hooks or formats
